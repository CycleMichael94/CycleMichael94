About

I am currently working toward a Bachelor of Business Associate with a specialization in finance. After narrowing down my interests at Kwantlen Polytechnic, I decided to pursue my education full time at Trinity Western University.

I am a hard working and ambitious employee who invests personally in any company in which I am placed. I am a natural leader who knows how to create task flows and delegate, but also knows how to work in a team environment.

A person with diverse experiences and an even more diverse portfolio of interests, I draw from many cultures and world views in order to connect with customers and co-workers to solve problems effectively and efficiently.

Beyond finance, I am passionate about specialty coffee and film photography. While these passions rarely merge with the world of finance I am always looking for intersections between the two. The time, attention and science behind a carefully-made cup of coffee and the deliberateness of film photography are what draw me to these topics.

Although I have learned lots while at Starbucks, my passion for coffee extends much further back. From my first coffee in a french press to experimenting with Chemex, cold brew, aeropress, and home espresso machines, my intrigue has only grown. I am constantly on the lookout for new information, soaking up every coffee-related video, podcast, article, and experience I possibly can.

While I (currently) do not have any certifications relating to coffee, I try to learn more every day about different preparation techniques, cupping, grading, varietals and their characteristics, and roasting.

As I mentioned above, I am always looking for ways in which my education in Finance and passion for specialty coffee can mix, and I believe that my experiences, knowledge, and skills are are useful in understanding more that just making coffee, but also the business side of running a shop and delivering value to customers and clients.
